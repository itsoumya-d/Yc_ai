# LegalForge -- Revenue Model

## Pricing Strategy

LegalForge uses a **per-seat, tiered subscription model** -- the standard pricing structure for B2B legal tech. Pricing is designed to capture value proportional to team size and usage complexity, with a clear upgrade path from mid-market to enterprise.

### LTV:CAC Ratio

| Metric              | Value   | Benchmark     |
| ------------------- | ------- | ------------- |
| LTV:CAC (per seat)  | 13.4x   | >3x is good   |
| LTV:CAC (per customer) | 66x | Exceptional    |
| CAC Payback Period   | 5 months| <12 months ideal |

The extremely high LTV:CAC ratio reflects two realities of legal tech:
1. **Switching costs are massive**: Migrating a contract repository, retraining AI on company playbooks, and retraining users makes churn very costly for customers
2. **Legal teams grow into the product**: Once adopted, usage expands from one contract type to all, from one practice area to all, and from one geography to all

## Customer Acquisition Strategy

### Channel Strategy

#### 1. CLOC / ACC Conferences (Primary - 25% of pipeline)

**Strategy**: Sponsor and exhibit at the two most important legal operations conferences. These events concentrate LegalForge's exact buyer persona.

**Tactics:**
- Booth with live AI drafting demos (show contract generated in real-time)
- Speaking sessions on "AI in Contract Management: Lessons from Early Adopters"
- GC roundtable dinners (8-10 GCs, intimate setting, thought leadership)
- Collect leads with ROI calculator (input team size, contract volume, get time/cost savings estimate)

**Budget**: $50K-100K per conference (sponsorship + travel + dinner events)
**Expected pipeline**: 30-50 qualified opportunities per conference

#### 2. LinkedIn Ads to GCs and Legal Ops (20% of pipeline)

**Strategy**: Highly targeted LinkedIn campaigns to General Counsel, VP Legal, Head of Legal Ops, and Contract Manager titles at companies with 500-10,000 employees.

**Tactics:**
- Sponsored content: case studies, ROI data, industry reports
- Lead gen forms: "Download: 2026 Contract Management Benchmark Report"
- InMail campaigns to identified accounts (ABM approach)
- Retargeting website visitors with product demo offers

**Budget**: $15K-30K/month
**Expected CAC**: $800 per qualified lead

#### 3. Content Marketing / SEO (25% of pipeline)

**Strategy**: Build organic authority in legal tech through high-quality content that legal professionals actively search for.

**Content Pillars:**
- "State of Contract Management" annual report (gated, lead gen)
- Blog: contract drafting best practices, AI in legal, clause comparison guides
- Webinars: monthly with GC guests discussing contract efficiency
- Case studies: quantified ROI from design partners
- Legal AI newsletter: weekly, builds audience of 10K+ legal professionals
- Template library: free contract templates (lead magnet, SEO)

**Budget**: $10K-20K/month (writer, designer, SEO tools)
**Expected timeline**: 6-12 months to meaningful organic traffic

#### 4. Referral / Word of Mouth (15% of pipeline)

**Strategy**: Legal is a relationship-driven industry. GCs talk to other GCs. Build a referral engine.

**Tactics:**
- Customer referral program: 1 month free for both referrer and referee
- GC Advisory Board: 5-10 GCs who advise on product and refer peers
- Legal ops community Slack channels: active participation, not selling
- Bar association and legal ops association partnerships

**Budget**: $5K/month (referral credits, advisory board dinners)

#### 5. Law Firm Partnerships (10% of pipeline)

**Strategy**: Partner with mid-size law firms whose corporate practices serve the same clients LegalForge targets. Firms recommend LegalForge to clients for routine contract work.

**Tactics:**
- Referral commission: 15% of first-year revenue for referred customers
- Co-marketing: joint webinars, co-branded content
- Integration: law firms use LegalForge to collaborate with clients on shared contracts
- Training: teach associates to use LegalForge, creating future in-house champions

**Budget**: $10K/month (commission payouts, co-marketing)

#### 6. Outbound Enterprise Sales (5% of pipeline)

**Strategy**: Direct outreach to Fortune 2000 legal departments for enterprise deals. High touch, high value.

**Tactics:**
- Account-based marketing (ABM) targeting top 200 accounts
- Multi-threaded outreach: GC + Legal Ops + IT Security
- Executive briefings and customized demos
- Proof-of-concept pilots (30-60 days)
- Security questionnaire and compliance documentation ready

**Budget**: $20K/month (AE salary allocation, ABM tools)

## Churn Analysis

### Expected Churn Rates

| Tier          | Monthly Churn | Annual Churn | Rationale                          |
| ------------- | ------------- | ------------ | ---------------------------------- |
| Starter       | 3.5%          | 35%          | Price-sensitive, evaluating tools  |
| Professional  | 1.5%          | 17%          | Invested in workflows, moderate switching cost |
| Enterprise    | 0.5%          | 6%           | Deep integration, massive switching cost |
| **Blended**   | **1.8%**      | **20%**      | Weighted by revenue contribution   |

### Why CLM Churn is Low

1. **Data lock-in**: Entire contract repository stored in LegalForge. Migration requires exporting, reformatting, and re-importing hundreds or thousands of contracts
2. **Workflow dependency**: Approval chains, clause libraries, templates, and team permissions are configured over months. Rebuilding in a new tool is extremely costly
3. **AI training**: Enterprise customers invest in custom AI model training on their playbooks. This training is not portable
4. **User training**: Legal professionals are slow to adopt new tools. Once trained, switching creates significant productivity loss during transition
5. **Compliance**: Audit logs and version histories are compliance artifacts. Migration risks compliance gaps

### Churn Mitigation Strategies

- **Quarterly Business Reviews (QBR)**: With Professional and Enterprise customers, review usage metrics, ROI, and roadmap alignment
- **Health Scoring**: Automated monitoring of usage patterns (login frequency, AI usage, contract volume). Alert CSM when health score drops
- **Champion Tracking**: Monitor when the internal champion (usually Legal Ops) changes roles. Proactive outreach to new contact
- **Feature Adoption**: Track which features each customer uses. Proactive enablement of unused features
- **Annual Contract Incentives**: 16% discount for annual commitment. Reduces monthly churn check-in points

As the contract repository grows, LegalForge becomes a data platform:

1. **Contract Intelligence API**: Third-party developers build on LegalForge's contract data and AI capabilities. Usage-based pricing: $0.01/API call

3. **Marketplace**: Third-party template packs, clause libraries, and playbooks sold through LegalForge. 20% platform commission
4. **Professional Services**: Implementation, custom model training, and integration services for enterprise. $200-400/hour
