### Annual Pricing (Discount)

| Tier | Monthly | Annual (per month) | Annual Total | Savings |
| ---- | ------- | ------------------ | ------------ | ------- |
| Navigator | $14.99/mo | $11.99/mo | $143.88/year | 20% |
| Pro | $29.99/mo | $23.99/mo | $287.88/year | 20% |

### Pricing Rationale

- **$14.99/mo Navigator:** Below the psychological $15 barrier. Cheaper than Netflix. Accessible to budget-conscious career changers. Core value (resume rewriting + full job matching) justifies the cost.
- **$29.99/mo Pro:** Still under $1/day. Mentor matching and interview prep are high-value, high-cost features that justify the premium. Users at this stage are actively job hunting and willing to invest.
- **Free tier is generous:** Skills assessment and 3 career paths for free creates a strong aha moment. Users see the value before being asked to pay. This is critical for our audience who may be skeptical of tech products.

## B2B2C: Employer Plans

### Pricing

| Plan | Price | Includes |
| ---- | ----- | -------- |
| **Starter** | $500/mo | 10 active job postings, access to candidate pool (anonymized), "Career Changer Friendly" badge, basic analytics |
| **Growth** | $1,500/mo | 50 active postings, priority placement in job matches, dedicated account manager, advanced analytics, employer branding page |
| **Enterprise** | Custom ($3K-10K/mo) | Unlimited postings, API access to candidate pool, custom matching criteria, co-branded landing pages, recruitment events |

### Employer Value Proposition

1. **Access motivated candidates.** Career changers who have invested months in upskilling are among the most motivated hires.
2. **Pre-vetted skills.** Transferability scores and completed coursework provide evidence of capability beyond traditional resumes.
3. **Diversity pipeline.** Career changers are demographically diverse, helping employers meet DEI goals.
4. **Reduced training cost.** SkillBridge users arrive with foundational skills already built.
5. **Brand as career-changer-friendly.** Public commitment to hiring career changers is a positive employer brand signal.

## Churn Analysis

### Expected Churn by Segment

| Segment | Monthly Churn | Reason | Mitigation |
| ------- | ------------- | ------ | ---------- |
| **Successful transitioners** | High (one-time event) | They got a new job -- goal accomplished | Celebrate success, offer alumni community, ask for testimonial, re-engage for future transitions |
| **Active learners** | Low (3-4%) | Engaged in learning plan, seeing progress | Progress tracking, streak motivation, course recommendations |
| **Job seekers** | Medium (5-7%) | Need results (interviews, offers) to stay motivated | Job match quality, application tracking, interview prep |
| **Stalled users** | High (10-15%) | Lost motivation, life got in the way | Re-engagement emails, mentor outreach, reduced plan options |
| **Free tier** | N/A | Not paying, but may convert later | Nurture with value, periodic upgrade prompts |

### Churn Reduction Strategies

1. **Time-to-value acceleration:** Users who complete assessment AND view career matches in first session have 3x higher retention
2. **Weekly progress emails:** Users who open weekly emails have 2x lower churn
3. **Mentor engagement:** Users matched with mentors have 40% lower churn
4. **Community participation:** Forum-active users have 50% lower churn
5. **7-day free trial for Pro:** Let users experience full value before committing
6. **Pause subscription option:** Instead of canceling, let users pause for 1-3 months (life circumstances)
7. **Annual plan incentives:** 20% discount drives commitment, reduces churn by 30-40%
8. **Success-based pricing (future):** Income share agreement option for users who can't afford subscription

### Employer Marketplace (Placement Fees)

When an employer hires a SkillBridge user:
- Placement fee: 5-10% of first-year salary (vs 15-25% for traditional recruiters)
- Average placement: $45,000 salary x 7.5% = $3,375 per placement
- 100 placements/month at scale = $337,500/month additional revenue
- This creates a flywheel: more successful placements attract more employers, which attract more users

### Net Revenue Retention

Target: 120% net revenue retention (NRR) through:
- Individual users upgrading Free -> Navigator -> Pro
- Employers upgrading Starter -> Growth -> Enterprise
- Government pilots converting to full deployments
- Affiliate revenue growing as user base grows

## Risk Factors and Mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Probability | Mitigation |
| ---- | ------ | ----------- | ---------- |
| OpenAI API costs increase significantly | High | Medium | Build abstraction layer, test alternative providers (Claude, Gemini, open-source) |
| Low free-to-paid conversion | High | Medium | A/B test paywall placement, add more free value, reduce friction |
| Government sales cycles too long | Medium | High | Focus on individual B2C revenue first; government is additive, not dependent |
| Competitor enters with more funding | Medium | Medium | Moat is data + AI + employer network. First-mover advantage in skills taxonomy |
| Recession reduces employer hiring | High | Low-Medium | Counter-cyclical: more displaced workers = more users. Shift revenue to government contracts |
| Users can't afford $14.99/mo | Medium | Medium | Reduced-cost plans for WIOA participants, income-based pricing tier, scholarship fund |
| Churn higher than projected | High | Medium | Invest in community, mentor matching, alumni network to maintain engagement post-transition |
