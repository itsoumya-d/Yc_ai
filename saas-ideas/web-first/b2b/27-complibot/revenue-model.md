# CompliBot -- Revenue Model

## Revenue Strategy

CompliBot monetizes through subscription pricing with three tiers designed to capture startups at every stage of their compliance journey. The pricing philosophy is simple: charge 60-70% less than Vanta, Drata, and Secureframe while delivering 80% of the functionality through AI automation. This price disruption opens the early-stage startup segment that incumbent players ignore because their cost structure requires $8K-50K annual contracts to achieve profitability.

### Revenue Composition

| Revenue Type             | Contribution | Description                                          |
| ------------------------ | ------------ | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Subscription (MRR)       | 85%          | Monthly/annual plan fees across three tiers          |
| Expansion Revenue        | 10%          | Framework additions, employee count upgrades         |
| Professional Services    | 5%           | Audit prep workshops, custom integration setup       |

### Lifetime Value (LTV)

```
Average Revenue per Customer (ARPC):
  Startup (40%):    $299/mo * 40% = $119.60
  Growth (40%):     $599/mo * 40% = $239.60
  Enterprise (20%): $999/mo * 20% = $199.80
  Blended ARPC: $559/mo

Average Customer Lifespan:
  Monthly churn: 1.5% (blended)
  Lifespan: 1 / 0.015 = 66.7 months (5.6 years)

  Note: Compliance is inherently sticky. Once a company achieves SOC 2
  through CompliBot, switching costs are enormous (re-uploading evidence,
  re-mapping controls, retraining auditor on new platform).

Gross Margin: 82% (after API costs, hosting, support)

LTV = ARPC * Lifespan * Gross Margin
LTV = $559 * 66.7 * 0.82 * (1 + 0.25 expansion)
    = $559 * 66.7 * 0.82 * 1.25
    = $38,230

Conservative LTV (using 3-year cap instead of full lifespan):
LTV = $559 * 36 * 0.82 * 1.25
    = $20,625

Ultra-conservative LTV (using 2-year cap, no expansion):
LTV = $559 * 24 * 0.82
    = $10,764
```

### LTV:CAC Ratio

| Calculation Method   | LTV      | CAC  | LTV:CAC | Assessment                  |
| -------------------- | -------- | ---- | ------- | --------------------------- |
| Full lifespan        | $38,230  | $600 | 63.7x   | Theoretical maximum         |
| 3-year cap           | $20,625  | $600 | 34.4x   | Optimistic but reasonable   |
| 2-year cap (no exp.) | $10,764  | $600 | 17.9x   | Ultra-conservative baseline |

Industry benchmark for healthy SaaS: LTV:CAC > 3x. CompliBot's ultra-conservative ratio of 17.9x indicates extremely healthy unit economics driven by low CAC (content marketing + referrals) and low churn (compliance is ongoing, switching costs are high).

### CAC Payback Period

```
CAC Payback = CAC / (ARPC * Gross Margin)
            = $600 / ($559 * 0.82)
            = $600 / $458
            = 1.3 months
```

A 1.3-month payback period is exceptional. For context, the SaaS industry median is 15-18 months. This rapid payback is driven by the low blended CAC from organic and referral channels. Even if paid acquisition scales and blended CAC rises to $1,200, the payback period remains under 3 months.

## Churn Analysis

### Why Compliance SaaS Has Structurally Low Churn

Compliance is not a one-time project. It is an ongoing obligation. This creates natural retention that most SaaS products cannot replicate:

1. **SOC 2 Type II requires continuous monitoring**: Type II audits cover a 3-12 month observation period. Canceling CompliBot mid-observation period means losing all collected evidence and starting over.

2. **Annual audits**: SOC 2 and ISO 27001 require annual re-certification. GDPR and HIPAA have ongoing requirements. Customers need CompliBot every year.

3. **Switching costs are enormous**: A company's entire compliance posture (policies, evidence, control mappings, audit history) lives in CompliBot. Migrating to a competitor means re-uploading hundreds of evidence items, re-mapping controls, and retraining the auditor on a new platform.

4. **Compliance scope expands**: As companies grow, they add frameworks (SOC 2 first, then GDPR for EU expansion, then HIPAA for health tech customers). This drives expansion rather than churn.

5. **Regulatory pressure only increases**: No company has ever needed less compliance over time. New regulations (state privacy laws, AI governance) continuously expand the compliance surface area.

### Net Revenue Retention (NRR)

```
NRR = (Starting MRR + Expansion - Contraction - Churn) / Starting MRR

Year 1 NRR:
  Starting MRR: $100 (indexed)
  Expansion: +$15 (15% of customers expand)
  Contraction: -$3 (3% downgrade)
  Churn: -$2 (2% monthly churn)
  NRR = ($100 + $15 - $3 - $2) / $100 = 110%

Year 2 NRR:
  Starting MRR: $100 (indexed)
  Expansion: +$30 (30% of customers expand, more frameworks)
  Contraction: -$2 (2% downgrade)
  Churn: -$1.5 (1.5% monthly churn)
  NRR = ($100 + $30 - $2 - $1.5) / $100 = 126.5%

Year 3+ NRR Target: 130%+
```

NRR above 120% indicates the business can grow revenue even with zero new customer acquisition. This is driven by the natural expansion path: companies grow, add frameworks, and upgrade tiers.
