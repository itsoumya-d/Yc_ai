# FieldLens

**Your AI mentor on every job site.**

`B2C` | `Mobile-First` | `AI for Physical Work`

---

## Elevator Pitch

FieldLens is an AI-powered mobile app that gives skilled tradespeople (plumbers, electricians, HVAC techs, carpenters) real-time coaching through their phone camera. Point your camera at any job, and FieldLens delivers step-by-step guidance, catches errors before they become callbacks, and confirms code compliance -- all hands-free with voice interaction. It turns every solo job into one with an expert looking over your shoulder. For the 11.5 million tradespeople in the US working without immediate access to a mentor, FieldLens replaces guesswork with confidence and cuts rework by up to 40%.

## YC Alignment

### RFS #7: AI for Physical Work (Spring 2026)

FieldLens maps directly to Y Combinator's Request for Startups #7, "AI for Physical Work." YC explicitly calls for startups using multimodal AI models to provide real-time coaching and guidance for physical tasks in the real world, exactly what FieldLens does.

### Jared Friedman's Vertical AI Thesis

Jared Friedman has argued that the next wave of massive outcomes will come from vertical AI applications -- AI tools purpose-built for specific industries rather than horizontal platforms. FieldLens embodies this thesis: it is not a general-purpose vision tool but an AI system deeply trained on trade-specific procedures, building codes, and common failure modes. The vertical specificity is the moat.

### Garry Tan on Trades + Technology

Garry Tan has repeatedly highlighted that the skilled trades represent one of the largest underserved markets in technology. The trades workforce is aging, the training pipeline is broken, and the workers themselves are increasingly tech-savvy millennials and Gen Z who expect mobile-first tools. Tan has noted that companies serving "the real economy" -- construction, trades, logistics -- are consistently undervalued by Silicon Valley despite massive TAMs. FieldLens sits squarely in this opportunity.

## Market Opportunity

### The Trades Crisis

- **723,000 new workers needed annually** in the US construction and trades industry (National Association of Home Builders)
- **11.5 million skilled tradespeople** currently active in the US (Bureau of Labor Statistics)
- **Average age of a plumber: 47 years old** -- retirements are accelerating faster than new entrants
- **70% of contractors** report difficulty finding qualified workers (Associated General Contractors of America)
- **$1.36 trillion** in US construction spending (2024), all of which depends on skilled labor

### Why Now

1. **GPT-4o and multimodal AI** make real-time visual analysis of physical work possible for the first time
2. **Trade worker demographics are shifting** -- younger workers (22-35) expect mobile tools and are comfortable with AI
3. **Trade school enrollment is declining** while demand is surging, creating a training gap that technology can fill
4. **Smartphone cameras** now have the resolution and processing power for real-time analysis in varied lighting conditions
5. **The 2024-2026 infrastructure boom** (CHIPS Act, IRA, Infrastructure Act) is creating unprecedented demand for trade labor

## Competition

| Competitor | Real-Time | AI-Powered | Hands-Free | Code Compliance | Mobile-First | Cost |
|-----------|-----------|------------|------------|----------------|-------------|------|
| **YouTube tutorials** | No | No | No | Rarely | Yes | Free |
| **Trade school** | No (classroom) | No | N/A | Yes | No | $5K-$30K |
| **Senior colleagues** | Yes | No | Yes | Varies | N/A | Unavailable at scale |
| **Procore (B2B)** | No | No | No | Partial | Yes | $375+/mo |
| **ChatGPT (general)** | No visual | Partial | No | No | Yes | $20/mo |
| **FieldLens** | **Yes** | **Yes (Vision)** | **Yes (Voice)** | **Yes** | **Yes** | **$29/mo** |

**Key insight:** There is no direct AI competitor providing real-time visual coaching for skilled trades. The market is entirely greenfield. The closest alternatives are either not real-time (YouTube), not AI-powered (trade school), not scalable (senior colleagues), or not trade-specific (ChatGPT).
