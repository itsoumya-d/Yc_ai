# Revenue Model

## Subscription Tiers

### Free -- $0/month

**Purpose:** Low-friction entry point. Let users experience the emotional value of the AI conversation and understand the depth of their digital footprint before committing financially.

**Included:**
- Basic wishes document (1 AI conversation covering one topic)
- 3 digital account entries
- 0 document vault storage (view recommended document checklist only)
- 1 trusted contact (no role-based permissions)
- No dead man's switch
- No legal templates
- No DocuSign integration
- Basic progress dashboard

**Why These Limits:** The free tier gives users enough to experience the AI conversation quality and understand the value of documenting their digital life. The 3-account limit is deliberately constraining for anyone with a real digital footprint (the average person has 100+ online accounts). The locked vault creates the strongest upgrade trigger -- users who complete a conversation naturally want to store the resulting document.

---

### Premium -- $9.99/month ($99.99/year)

**Purpose:** Full individual experience. Everything a single person needs for complete end-of-life planning.

**Included:**
- Unlimited AI conversation sessions across all topics
- Unlimited digital account entries with encrypted credential storage
- Unlimited document vault storage (up to 10GB)
- 5 trusted contacts with role-based permissions (executor, healthcare proxy, digital executor)
- Dead man's switch with full escalation chain
- Legal templates for 10 states (expanding to all 50)
- 2 DocuSign signings per year
- Document AI recognition and categorization
- Encrypted data export
- Priority support

**Annual Discount:** $99.99/year (17% savings, effectively $8.33/month). Annual plans reduce churn and improve LTV.

**Why $9.99:** Below the $10 psychological threshold. Cheaper than estate planning attorneys ($1,000-3,000). Competitive with password managers ($3-5/month) and cloud storage ($3-10/month), which are analogous "set it and forget it" subscriptions. Low enough that users don't cancel when they feel "done" because the dead man's switch provides continuous ongoing value.

---

### Family -- $19.99/month ($199.99/year)

**Purpose:** Coordinated family planning. The highest-value tier because it creates network effects that dramatically reduce churn for all members.

**Included:**
- Everything in Premium for up to 5 family members
- Shared family vault for common documents (joint property deeds, marriage certificates, family insurance)
- Each member maintains their own private vault
- 10 trusted contacts per member with role-based permissions
- Family progress dashboard (opt-in visibility of each member's completion)
- Joint document templates (complementary wills for couples)
- All available state legal templates
- 5 DocuSign signings per year (per family)
- Family admin billing and member management
- Coordinated dead man's switch (family members can verify each other's check-ins)

**Annual Discount:** $199.99/year (17% savings, effectively $16.67/month).

**Why $19.99:** Effective per-member cost of $4/month (5 members) makes it a clear bargain. Couples who plan together have estimated 50% lower churn. Family plans are harder to cancel (requires consensus, affects multiple people). Creates natural word-of-mouth within extended families.

## Unit Economics

### Conversion Rate: 6% Free-to-Paid

| Funnel Stage | Conversion | Running Total (per 100K downloads) |
|---|---|---|
| App download | 100% | 100,000 |
| Complete onboarding | 65% | 65,000 |
| First AI conversation | 45% | 45,000 |
| Hit free tier limit | 30% | 30,000 |
| View upgrade prompt | 25% | 25,000 |
| Start trial or subscribe | 8% | 8,000 |
| Convert to paid (75% trial conversion) | 6% | 6,000 |

**Key conversion drivers:**
1. Vault lock after AI conversation (user generates document but can't save it)
2. 3-account limit hit after adding 3 familiar services (Google, Facebook, bank)
3. Dead man's switch only available on paid tiers
4. Legal templates framed as "$2,000+ savings vs. attorney"

### Monthly Churn: 3%

| Segment | Monthly Churn |
|---|---|
| Premium Monthly | 4.5% |
| Premium Annual | 1.5% |
| Family Monthly | 2.5% |
| Family Annual | 0.8% |
| **Blended** | **3.0%** |

**Why churn is exceptionally low:**
1. **Extreme switching costs.** Migrating encrypted documents, digital asset inventory, and trusted contact configurations to a competitor is enormously painful.
2. **Dead man's switch dependency.** Canceling deactivates the switch. This ongoing value cannot be "taken with you."
3. **High emotional investment.** Users spent hours in deeply personal AI conversations. The data represents irreplaceable wishes.
4. **Family plan network effects.** One member wanting to cancel affects all members. Family consensus to cancel is rare.
5. **Accumulating value.** Unlike fitness apps where engagement peaks and fades, estate planning data only grows more valuable over time as more documents, accounts, and contacts are added.

**Churn by phase:**
- Month 1-3: 8% (trial/exploration phase)
- Month 4-12: 4% (settling in, storing real documents)
- Month 13+: 1.5% (deeply embedded, high switching cost)
- Weighted average lifetime: ~40 months

### CAC: $15

| Channel | CAC | % of Acquisitions |
|---|---|---|
| Organic search (SEO/content marketing) | $5 | 35% |
| App Store organic (ASO) | $3 | 20% |
| PR and earned media | $8 | 15% |
| Life insurance partnerships (co-marketing) | $12 | 10% |
| Paid social (Facebook, Instagram retargeting) | $35 | 10% |
| Referrals (word of mouth) | $2 | 10% |
| **Blended CAC** | **$15** | **100%** |

### LTV: $560

```
LTV = ARPU x Average Lifetime
LTV = $14 x 40 months
LTV = $560
```

### LTV:CAC: 37:1

```
LTV:CAC = $560 / $15 = 37.3x
```

SaaS benchmark for a healthy business is 3:1. Mortal's exceptional ratio results from low CAC (content marketing dominance), high LTV (extremely low churn + reasonable ARPU), and minimal direct competition.

### Payback Period: 1 Month

```
Payback = CAC / ARPU = $15 / $14 = 1.07 months
```

CAC recovered in the first month of paid subscription. This means aggressive growth requires minimal working capital for customer acquisition.

## Acquisition Channels

### 1. Content Marketing -- "Digital Legacy" (35% of acquisitions)

**Strategy:** Become the definitive online resource for digital estate planning.

**Content Pillars:**
| Pillar | Example Topics | Monthly Search Volume |
|---|---|---|
| Estate Planning 101 | "How to write a will without a lawyer" | 50K-200K |
| Digital Legacy | "What happens to social media when you die" | 10K-50K |
| Medical Directives | "Living will vs advance directive" | 20K-80K |
| Life Planning | "End of life planning checklist" | 5K-30K |

**Content calendar:** 3-4 posts/week. 1 downloadable guide/month. Monthly newsletter "The Legacy Letter."

**SEO targets:** 50K visitors/month (month 6), 150K (month 12), 500K (month 18).

### 2. Life Insurance Partnerships (10% of acquisitions)

**Target partners:** Ladder, Haven Life, Bestow, Ethos (digital-first insurers).

**Value for insurers:** Mortal users are pre-qualified leads -- actively thinking about death and legacy, likely underinsured, with quantified assets.

**Revenue model:** $50-200 per policy sold through referral. Estimated 0.5% of users generate insurance referral revenue.

### 3. PR -- Topic Generates Strong Media Interest (15% of acquisitions)

**Why PR works for Mortal:** Death, legacy, and "what happens to your digital life" are inherently compelling media topics. The human interest angle is strong and generates organic sharing.

**Target publications:** TechCrunch (tech angle), Forbes/CNBC (financial angle), NPR/The Atlantic (human interest), AARP (demographic fit).

**Seasonal hooks:** National Estate Planning Awareness Week (October), Tax Day (April), New Year planning season (January).

### 4. Apple/Google Feature Potential (part of ASO, 20%)

**Why Mortal could be featured:** Design quality (empathetic UX is unusual), social impact (helping families), privacy-first architecture (Apple values this), novel AI application, accessibility focus.

### 5. Referral Program (10% of acquisitions)

Referrer gets 1 free month. Referred user gets 30-day extended trial. Viral coefficient: 0.15 (each user brings 0.15 additional users).

### 6. Paid Social -- Retargeting Only Initially (10% of acquisitions)

Facebook/Instagram retargeting of blog visitors and app page viewers. $0/month for months 1-6, scaling to $5K/month by month 18. Maintain CAC below $35 for paid channels.

## Target Demographics

### Primary: Adults 35-65 with Digital Assets and Dependents

- **Tech-savvy parents** (35-50): Have children, multiple online accounts, some crypto or investment accounts, aware they should plan but haven't
- **Digital natives entering estate planning age** (35-45): First generation with more digital property than physical property, 100+ online accounts, crypto holdings
- **Pre-retirees** (50-65): Thinking about legacy, have accumulated both digital and physical assets, motivated by desire to protect family
- **Crypto holders** (any age): Acutely aware that lost private keys mean lost assets forever, high urgency for digital estate planning
- **Multi-account digital natives** (30-45): People with complex digital lives -- multiple email accounts, cloud storage, subscriptions, social media presences

### Why This Demographic:

1. **Willingness to pay:** This age range has disposable income and understands the value of planning
2. **Digital complexity:** Enough online accounts and digital assets to make the product immediately useful
3. **Dependents create urgency:** Having children, aging parents, or a spouse creates natural motivation
4. **Smartphone-native:** Comfortable with mobile-first apps and in-app purchases
5. **Insurance awareness:** Already thinking about protection (life insurance, health insurance) -- estate planning is a natural extension

### Why Mortal Reaches $1M MRR

1. **Massive underserved market.** 67% of Americans (175 million adults) have no estate plan. The digital asset angle is almost entirely unaddressed.
2. **Low churn creates compounding growth.** Each cohort retains at high rates, so monthly revenue from past cohorts stacks on top of new acquisition.

4. **Family plans create network effects.** Each family member reduces churn for all members and becomes an evangelist.
5. **Multiple expansion paths.** Attorney marketplace, insurance partnerships, enterprise benefits, and ultra-premium tiers provide revenue growth beyond user count growth.
6. **No well-funded competitor.** No mobile-first, AI-powered, empathy-designed competitor exists. Traditional estate planning is dominated by expensive attorneys and clunky form-based tools.
