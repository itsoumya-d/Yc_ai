# Mortal

**The most important app you'll ever set up.**

`B2C` | `Mobile-First` | `End-of-Life Tech`

---

## Elevator Pitch

Mortal helps people organize their end-of-life wishes, digital legacy, and legal documents through empathetic AI-guided conversations. Instead of facing a stack of legal forms alone, users talk through their wishes with an AI that feels like a thoughtful friend — covering everything from funeral preferences and organ donation to what happens to their Instagram, crypto wallets, and Netflix account when they're gone.

The result: a comprehensive, encrypted, legally-aware plan that trusted contacts can access when the time comes — including a "dead man's switch" that ensures nothing falls through the cracks.

## YC Alignment

**Theme: "Technology for End-of-Life Care & Human Experience"**

Mortal sits at the intersection of three YC-backed theses:

1. **Reducing long-term care costs** — Proper end-of-life planning reduces family legal disputes (which cost $10K-$50K+ on average) and prevents unnecessary medical interventions that cost the healthcare system billions annually.
2. **Combating loneliness in aging populations** — The AI conversation flow creates a guided, non-judgmental space for people to process end-of-life decisions, reducing the emotional isolation around a universally taboo topic.
3. **Improving end-of-life planning** — Digitizing and simplifying a process that currently requires $2,500+ in attorney fees and weeks of emotional labor.

**Prior YC Companies in Adjacent Space:**

- **Lantern (W16)** — End-of-life planning platform. Pre-AI era, relied on static content and checklists. Proved market demand exists but lacked the conversational intelligence to guide users through emotionally difficult decisions.
- **Cake** — End-of-life planning startup, later acquired by Affirm. Validated that fintech-adjacent end-of-life tools attract acquisition interest from major financial platforms.

Mortal is the first AI-native approach to this space — built for a generation that has more digital assets than physical ones.

## Market Opportunity

### The Problem is Massive and Underserved

- **67% of Americans have no estate plan** (Caring.com, 2024 survey). That's roughly 170 million adults without basic end-of-life documentation.
- **The first generation of digital natives is entering estate planning age.** Millennials (now 28-43) have an average of 100+ online accounts, crypto holdings, cloud-stored documents, and social media presences that traditional estate planning completely ignores.
- **Digital assets are the new forgotten estate.** The average American has $35,000+ in digital assets (including crypto, digital media, online accounts with monetary value) that have no succession plan.
- **COVID-19 permanently shifted attitudes.** Post-pandemic, interest in estate planning among adults under 45 increased by 63% (Gallup, 2022), but conversion to actual plans remains below 15% — the tools don't meet them where they are.

### Why Now

1. **AI maturity** — GPT-4-class models can finally handle emotionally nuanced conversations with appropriate sensitivity, something impossible even 2 years ago.
2. **Digital asset explosion** — Crypto, NFTs, subscription services, cloud storage, social media accounts. The average person now has more digital "property" than physical property.
3. **Regulatory tailwind** — The Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act (RUFADAA) is being adopted state by state, creating a legal framework that didn't exist 5 years ago.
4. **Generational shift** — Gen X and Millennials prefer digital-first, mobile-first experiences over sitting in a lawyer's office.

## Competition

| Feature | Mortal | LegalZoom | Trust & Will | Everplans |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AI-guided conversations | Yes | No | No | No |
| Digital asset management | Yes | No | No | Partial |
| Document vault (encrypted) | Yes | No | Basic | Yes |
| Legal templates | Yes (AI-assisted) | Yes (form-based) | Yes (form-based) | No |
| Dead man's switch | Yes | No | No | No |
| Trusted contacts system | Yes | No | Basic | Yes |
| Mobile-first | Yes | No | Partial | No |
| Emotional design | Yes (core principle) | No | No | No |
| Pricing | $9.99/mo | $79-$249 one-time | $39-$159 one-time | B2B only |

### Why Competitors Can't Catch Up

- **LegalZoom** is a forms company. Their entire architecture is built around filling out legal documents. Adding conversational AI and digital asset management would require a ground-up rebuild of their core product.
- **Trust & Will** focuses exclusively on legal documents. They have no digital asset inventory, no dead man's switch, and no AI conversation layer. Their moat is legal template coverage, not user experience.
- **Everplans** pivoted to B2B (selling to financial advisors and funeral homes). They've abandoned the direct consumer market and their consumer UX has stagnated since 2019.
